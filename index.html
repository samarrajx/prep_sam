01
Incline DB Press MMA ✓ ›
Upper chest · Anterior delt · Punch angle strength
4×8-12
Cues
  • 30–45° bench; pinch shoulder blades before unracking
  • Lower to outer chest, elbows ~75° from torso
  • Drive through palms; rotate slightly inward at top
  • 2-sec eccentric; stop 1-2 shy of failure first 3 sets
MMA Relevance
The incline angle mirrors the upward punch trajectory. Building upper-chest and shoulder strength here directly improves your jab and cross power.
02
Flat Bench Press MMA ✓ ›
Mid/lower chest · Horizontal push strength
4×6-10
Cues
  • Natural arch, feet drive into floor for stability
  • Touch bar to lower sternum — not nipple line
  • Elbows 45–60° to torso; "pull the bar apart" cue
  • Full leg drive = more upper-body force output
Pro Tip
This is your horizontal pushing foundation. At 67 kg / 22 years old with 20 days exp., aim to add 2.5 kg per week. Track it. Numbers going up = you're growing.
03
Cable Fly ›
Chest isolation · Constant tension · Pump
3×12-15
Cues
  • Mid cable height; slight forward lean, soft elbow bend
  • "Hugging a barrel" arc — not a press
  • Squeeze pecs 1 sec at peak; embrace the stretch on return
Note
Kept from original plan — quality isolation exercise. As you progress into Phase 2, swap 1 set here for a medicine ball chest pass to add explosive component.
Triceps — Kept, functional priority
04
Rope Pushdown ›
Lateral + medial tricep · Lockout strength
3×10-15
Cues
  • Slight forward hinge; elbows pinned to sides
  • Split rope apart at bottom — full lateral-head squeeze
  • Rope up to chin level for full stretch each rep
Pro Tip
Triceps = 2/3 of arm mass. These are worth keeping. Higher reps, slow eccentrics, constant tension.
05
Overhead DB Extension ›
Long head (biggest head) · Overhead strength
3×10-12
Cues
  • Upper arms vertical, fixed; only forearms hinge
  • Lower until deep stretch behind head
  • Extend fully; squeeze at top
Pro Tip
Long head only stretches with arm overhead. Biggest tricep stimulus possible. Don't skip this.
Abs — Upgraded for MMA

Cable Crunch MMA ✓ ›
Loaded flexion · Progressive overload on abs
3×15
Cues
  • Kneel, rope behind head — don't pull with neck
  • Hinge at hips THEN crunch rib cage to pelvis
  • 1–2 sec hold at bottom; controlled return
MMA Link
A strong rectus absorbs body shots and generates power in the clinch. Add weight weekly — abs respond to progressive overload just like any muscle.
07
Decline Crunch ›
Burnout · Full rectus
2×Failure
Cues

  • Full stretch at bottom — true failure both sets

Replace one set with Hollow Body Hold ×30 sec — a fundamental grappling/gymnastics core position that translates directly to ground work.

Back — Width & Thickness
01
V-Bar Lat Pulldown MMA ✓ ›
Lower lats · Clinch pulls · Underhook fighting
4×8-12
Cues
  • Lean back 15°; pull to upper sternum
  • Depress scapula first — "shoulders into back pockets"
  • Full stretch at top every rep
MMA Link
Lat strength = your primary weapon in the clinch. Every underhook battle, every grip fight, every time you drag someone down — these muscles do the work.
02
Single Arm Lat Pulldown ›
Unilateral · Fixes imbalance
3×10
Cues
  • Start with weaker side; match reps exactly
  • Torso stable, no lateral lean to cheat
Note
As a beginner, left-right imbalances are extremely common and can hold you back. This exercise fixes them early before they become ingrained patterns.
03
T-Bar Row MMA ✓ ›
Mid-back thickness · Takedown base strength
3×8-12
Cues
  • Hip hinge 45°; back flat, chest proud
  • Pull to lower sternum; squeeze shoulder blades at top
  • Don't round upper back under load
MMA Link
The hip-hinge rowing position is almost identical to a double-leg takedown stance. You're building functional strength in an MMA-specific body position.
Biceps
04
Incline DB Curl ›
Long head · Maximum stretch position
3×10-12
Cues
  • 45–60° bench; arms hang fully behind body
  • Curl with supination — rotate pinky toward ceiling
  • No swing; upper arms fixed
Pro Tip
Best exercise for bicep peak. The stretched position creates maximum muscle damage and growth stimulus.
05
Close Grip Barbell Curl ›
Long head bias · Grip strength carry-over
3×8-12
Cues
  • Shoulder-width grip or narrower; elbows glued to sides
  • Full ROM: complete extension to full contraction
  • Zero body swing
Pro Tip
Strict form over weight. At your stage, the neural strength gains from perfect-form curls outweigh any benefit from heaving heavier weight.
Forearms — REDUCED (Phase 1: keep 2 sets only)
06
Reverse Curl MMA ✓ ›
Brachialis · Brachioradialis · Grip endurance for grappling
3×15
Cues
  • Overhand grip; elbows fixed to sides
  • Controlled tempo — forearm extensors are weaker than flexors
MMA Link
Grip endurance is one of the most underrated MMA attributes. Reverse curls build the brachioradialis — critical for holding clinches, breaking grips, and fighting off submission attempts.
07
⚡ NEW: Farmer's Carry MMA ✓ ›
Grip · Core stability · Mental toughness · Full-body conditioning
3×30m
Cues
  • Heavy DBs or kettlebells; stand tall, shoulders packed
  • Short quick steps; don't let torso sway
  • Breathe rhythmically; maintain grip to the end
  • 30 metres per set; rest 60 sec between
Why Added
Farmer's carries replaced wrist curls here. They build grip, core bracing, postural endurance, and mental toughness simultaneously — all MMA-critical. One of the best exercises you can do for combat sports.

Legs — Power Foundation
01
Squat / Leg Press MMA ✓ ›
Quads · Glutes · Fight stance power base
4×8-12
Squat Priority
  • Bar on mid-trap; brace core hard before descent
  • Break hips and knees simultaneously; descend to parallel+
  • Knees track over toes; drive through full foot
  • Build to Squat ≥ bodyweight (67 kg) by Phase 3
MMA Link
Every kick, every takedown, every explosive movement in MMA originates from leg drive. Prioritise squat over leg press for real-world strength transfer.
02
Romanian Deadlift MMA ✓ ›
Hamstrings · Glutes · Hip hinge pattern
3×8-12
Cues
  • Soft knee bend; hinge at hips not squat
  • Bar stays close; lower until hamstrings fully stretch
  • Drive hips forward; squeeze glutes at top
MMA Link
Hip extension power (RDL) is the biomechanical foundation of a rear kick, a hip throw, and explosive level changes. One of the most important exercises for combat athletes.
03
Leg Curl ›
Hamstring isolation · Balanced knee health
3×12
Cues
  • Hips flat; curl fully, hold briefly; 3-sec eccentric
Pro Tip
A hamstring that's weaker than the quad is an ACL injury waiting to happen — especially in MMA with its cutting, pivoting, and takedown movements. Keep this in.
Shoulders — Striking Power
04
Overhead Press MMA ✓ ›
Anterior delt · Clinch dominance · Underhook battles
4×6-10
Cues
  • Bar at clavicle; press up and slightly back over ears
  • Elbows slightly in front; tuck chin as bar passes face
  • Full overhead extension every rep
MMA Link
Every underhook battle, every time you create or break frames in the clinch, every time you push-kick — overhead press strength is directly expressed. Go heavy here.
05
Lateral Raise ›
Medial delt · Width · Guard position endurance
3×12-15
Cues
  • Lead with elbows; tilt pinkies slightly up at top
  • Stop at shoulder height; controlled descent
MMA Note
Keeping your guard up for 3 rounds requires medial delt endurance. High-rep laterals build exactly that capacity. Don't neglect them.
06
Rear Delt Fly MMA ✓ ›
Posterior delt · Rotator cuff health · Posture
3×12-15
Cues
  • Bend forward ~90°; slight elbow bend; drive elbows back and up
  • Hold 1 sec at top; don't use traps
Critical
Rear delts protect your shoulder joint. MMA involves heavy overhead loading, shot-taking, and ground work — a weak posterior shoulder is a labrum tear waiting to happen.
Abs — Anti-Rotation + Stability
07
Hanging Leg Raise MMA ✓ ›
Lower abs · Hip flexors · Guard retention
3×12-15
Cues
  • Hang from bar; raise legs to 90°+ with posterior pelvic tilt at top
  • The "hip curl" activates lower abs — not just hip flexors
MMA Link
Hip flexor + lower ab strength = keeping your guard active from your back. Crucial for BJJ and grappling defence.
08
Plank MMA ✓ ›
Deep core bracing · Anti-extension · Punch transfer
3×45s
Cues
  • Rigid body; squeeze glutes + quads + abs simultaneously
  • Breathe steadily; progress to 60–90s then weighted
MMA Link
Core stiffness is what transfers your leg drive into your fist. A loose core leaks power. A rigid, braced core transmits force. Every elite striker trains this.

3×10
Cues

  • Catch the rebound or pick up; this is explosive, not slow
Why Added
Replaced decline crunch on this day. Med-ball slams develop rotational core power, explosive hip extension, and conditioning simultaneously — pure MMA gold. Start Phase 1, stick with it.

⚡ Pull-Up Progression MMA ✓ ›
Relative strength benchmark · Full bodyweight pull
3×Max
Progressions

Benchmark
10 strict pull-ups at bodyweight (67 kg) = elite relative upper-body strength for a combat athlete. This is a key target before you walk into any MMA gym.

Legs — Glute + Functional Focus
01
Bulgarian Split Squat MMA ✓ ›
Single-leg strength · Balance · Fight stance mechanics
3×10
Cues
  • Rear foot on bench; front foot 2–3 steps out
  • Upright torso = quad focus; slight lean = glute focus
  • Drive through front heel; don't push off back foot
  • Start bodyweight; add DBs when solid
MMA Link
MMA is 100% single-leg sport when moving, shooting, or striking. This exercise builds the explosive single-leg stability you need. It will also fix whatever squat imbalances you have.
02
Hip Thrust MMA ✓ ›
Glutes · Hip extension power · Kick and throw force
3×10-12
Cues
  • Shoulder blades on bench; barbell across hips with pad
  • Drive to full extension; squeeze glutes hard at top
  • Tuck pelvis — don't hyperextend lower back
MMA Link
Hip thrust is literally training the explosive hip extension that powers kicks, throws, and ground-and-pound. Elite MMA athletes include hip thrusts for exactly this reason.
03
Leg Extension ›
Quad isolation · Knee health balance
3×15
Cues
  • Knee joint aligns with cam pivot; extend fully, squeeze VMO
  • 3-sec eccentric; toes slightly inward = more inner quad
Pro Tip
High reps here develop quad endurance needed for repeated kicks and stance maintenance throughout a full fight.
04
Calf Raises ›
Gastrocnemius · Soleus · Footwork base
4×20
Cues

  • Vary: straight leg (gastroc) and bent knee (soleus) sets
MMA Note
Calves absorb landing impact and power footwork in all directions. Most beginners never train them. Don't be one of them.
⚡ Day 6 — Main Conditioning Session (Most Important Day)
Phase 1 (Wk 1–5)
25 min steady jog at conversational pace. You should be able to hold a sentence. Build the aerobic base first.
Phase 2 (Wk 6–10)
Intervals: 30 sec sprint / 90 sec walk × 8 rounds. Then 3×2 min bag work (or shadow). Total ~30 min.
Phase 3 (Wk 11–16)
5×3 min rounds (bag + shadow + sprawl drill). 1 min rest between. This is a real fight simulation. Finish with 10 min cool-down jog.
